# What I Learned

Despite its notoriety, *National Review* was unprofitable for much of its history. The magazine's founder, William F. Buckley Jr., subsidized the magazine with $6 million of his own money over his lifetime. From [Buckley: The Life and the Revolution that Changed America](https://www.amazon.com/Buckley-Life-Revolution-Changed-America/dp/0375502343) (Sam Tanenhaus, 2025).

Amazon's decision to place Sponsored Listings at the top of search results led to bitter internal debates. While the advertising revenues from doing so were likely to be enormous, the company was concerned about upholding its commitment to be "the most customer-centric company on Earth." After internal analysis found that the change caused only a small decrease in sales, Jeff Bezos approved the change, reasoning that it was better to be a company that sold advertising with lower prices than one with no advertising and higher prices. From [Amazon Unbound: Jeff Bezos and the Invention of a Global Empire](https://www.amazon.com/Amazon-Unbound-Invention-Global-Empire/dp/1982132612) (Brad Stone, 2021).
